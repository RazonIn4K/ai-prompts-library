# Outreach First Line Prompt

## Purpose
This prompt helps generate compelling first lines for outreach messages that capture attention and establish connection.

## Voice and Style Guidelines

### Voice
- **Conversational yet professional**: Strike a balance between approachable and credible
- **Confident without arrogance**: Show expertise while remaining humble
- **Genuine and human**: Avoid overly salesy or robotic language
- **Empathetic**: Demonstrate understanding of the recipient's perspective

### Style
- **Personalized**: Reference specific details about the recipient or their work
- **Concise**: Get to the point quickly (1-2 sentences maximum)
- **Value-focused**: Hint at benefit or relevance immediately
- **Question or observation-based**: Engage curiosity rather than making demands
- **Avoid clichés**: No "I hope this email finds you well" or similar overused phrases

## Example Prompt

```
Write a compelling first line for an outreach email to [RECIPIENT] who [CONTEXT]. 
The line should be personalized, engaging, and immediately convey value. 
Avoid generic openings and focus on creating genuine connection.
```

## Sample Outputs

**For a marketing professional:**
"I noticed your recent campaign for [Brand] increased engagement by 40%—the storytelling approach you used is exactly what we're exploring for our new initiative."

**For a developer:**
"Your GitHub contribution to [Project] solved a problem I've been wrestling with for weeks, and I'd love to hear your thoughts on taking that concept further."

**For a business leader:**
"After reading your LinkedIn article on sustainable growth, I'm curious how you'd approach [specific challenge] that companies in your industry are facing."
